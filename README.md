# Ola Driver Churn Analysis

## Project Overview
This project aims to predict driver attrition (churn) for Ola, a leading ride-sharing service. High driver churn is a significant challenge in the industry, as acquiring new drivers is more expensive than retaining existing ones. 

We analyzed monthly driver data from 2019 and 2020 to identify patterns and build a machine learning model that predicts whether a driver will leave the company.

## Dataset Description
The dataset contains monthly snapshots of driver information, including:
- **Driver_ID**: Unique identifier for each driver.
- **MMM-YY**: Reporting month.
- **Age & Gender**: Demographic information.
- **City**: City of operation.
- **Education_Level**: 0 (Low) to 2 (High).
- **Income**: Monthly income.
- **Dateofjoining & LastWorkingDate**: Used to calculate tenure and churn status.
- **Joining Designation & Grade**: Hierarchy and performance metrics.
- **Total Business Value**: Revenue generated by the driver.
- **Quarterly Rating**: Performance rating (1 to 4).

## Key Insights from EDA
- **Churn Definition**: Churn is defined by the presence of a `LastWorkingDate`. Approximately 1.6k drivers churned in the period provided.
- **Age Distribution**: The workforce is dominated by individuals in their early to mid-30s.
- **Income Trends**: Drivers who joined earlier (higher tenure) tend to earn more and generate higher business value.
- **Performance Impact**: Drivers with a 'Quarterly Rating' of 1 are significantly more likely to churn (19% churn rate) compared to those with a rating of 2 or higher (1-3% churn rate).
- **Outliers**: Less than 1% of drivers are high-income outliers, while ~7% are high-value business contributors.

## Data Preprocessing
- **Missing Values**: Handled using median imputation for Age and mode for Gender.
- **Feature Engineering**:
    - Calculated `Tenure` using joining and last working dates.
    - Created a `Rating_Declining` flag (True if the last rating is lower than the average rating).
    - Aggregated data to the **Driver Level** to prevent snapshot bias.
- **Scaling**: Used `StandardScaler` for numeric features before training.

## Modeling and Results
We transitioned from a baseline Logistic Regression to an **Advanced Random Forest Classifier** to handle non-linear relationships and class imbalance.

### Model Performance:
- **Accuracy**: ~80.7%
- **F1-Score (Churned class)**: 0.87
- **Recall (Churned class)**: 0.91 (High sensitivity is crucial for identifying drivers at risk).

### Top Churn Predictors:
1. **Total Business Value**
2. **Average Quarterly Rating**
3. **Income**
4. **Tenure**
